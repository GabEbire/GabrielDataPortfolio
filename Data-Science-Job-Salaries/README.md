# Data Science Job Salaries Analysis Dashboard

## Project Overview

This project analyzes analyzes salary and workforce trends within the data science industry.

---

## Business Problem

The objective of this analysis is to address challenges organizations and job seekers often face by transforming raw salary data into actionable insights that support workforce planning, salary benchmarking, and hiring strategies

---

## Tools Used

- Power BI
- Microsoft Excel

---

## Dataset

The dataset contains survey records, including:

- Work Year
- Experience Level 
- Employment Type 
- Job Title
- Salary
- Salary Currency
- Salary in Usd
- Employee Residence
- Remote Ratio
- Company Location
- Company Size

---

## Data Cleaning

The following cleaning steps were performed:

- Removed duplicate records
- Corrected missing values
- Standardized column names
- Converted experience level placeholder to right format i.e EN to Entry-level, MI to Mid-level, SE to Senior-level and EX to Executive-level 
- Converted employment type placeholder to right format i.e PT to Part-time, FT to Full-time, CT to Contract and FL to Freelance 
- Verified data consistency

---

## Key KPIs

- Total Employees
- Total Job Titles
- Total Salary Paid
- Top Employing Role

---

## Key Insights

- Workforce Scale & Growth: Hiring scaled massively over time, growing from 46 employees in 2020 to 122 in 2021, reaching its peak in 2022.
- Role Domination: The top 3 most populated positions are Data Scientists (143), Data Engineers (132), and Data Analysts (97).
- Compensation by Experience: Financial investment is heavily skewed toward Mid-level talent ($27M) and Senior-level talent ($13M).
